Business Model
3.1 · Revenue model

Three revenue channels. Pension drives the compounding line.

Accruo has three revenue channels. The largest long-term contributor is an annual fee on assets under management, which compounds with every contribution. HR customers pay a per-employee subscription that activates on signup; individuals do not. And, as is customary in the market, Accruo earns a small commission on the health and life cover sold alongside the pension.

Stream 1 · AUM trail fee

AUM is the engine of the business because it compounds without further sales effort. Every monthly contribution adds to the asset base, every year of market growth widens the fee, and every employee who stays through a job change carries their PRSA balance with them. By Year 4, AUM trail accounts for around 70% of revenue.

Accruo charges 0.50% per year on assets under management, applied across both the workplace pension and the individual product. This is one-third below the closest Irish digital benchmark, Kota at 0.75%, and a small fraction of the European average. The lower fee is made possible by removing the double marginalisation of the broker model and being digital-first. It is not without precedent: most US and Canadian digital wealth managers charge at this level. The cost stack supports the price.

Stream 2 · Per-employee subscription

Accruo charges €7 per employee per month for platform access on the workplace pension. Larger firms are accustomed to paying per-employee subscription fees for HRIS platforms like Personio, and there is market precedent: Kota charges a similar fee for its platform.

Smaller firms are price-sensitive, so Accruo discounts to €3.50 per employee per month for them. This is the inverse of Kota's enterprise discounting, and aligns with platforms like Claude, which charge more for enterprise customers. Subscription revenue activates from day one, regardless of contribution levels or fund returns. In Year 2 it is a meaningful share of revenue; by Year 4 it has settled around 22% as AUM scales underneath it.

Stream 3 · Insurance commissions

Through its IIA licence, Accruo resells group health and group life cover from established Irish carriers and earns a 6% commission on premium, the standard rate across the Irish broker market. Average premiums are €1,500 per employee for health and €75 for life. Insurance is a small line on its own, around 7% of total revenue at scale, but it lets Accruo own the full employee-benefit relationship and develop an ecosystem that makes it less likely the customer will migrate.
