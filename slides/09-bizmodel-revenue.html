Business Model
3.1 · Revenue model

Three revenue streams. One that compounds.

Accruo earns revenue from a management fee on assets, a SaaS subscription fee from corporate customers, and commissions on brokered life and health insurance products.

Stream 1 · Management fees

Accruo charges 0.50% per year on all assets under management. That's roughly one-third the Irish average. And the fee compounds. Every contribution and every year of market growth widens it, making AUM the long-term engine of the business, accounting for around 70% of revenue by Year 4.

We can offer this rate by removing the double marginalisation of the broker model, running a digital-first platform that scales, and using lower-cost index funds in place of the actively managed funds incumbents prefer. It is not without precedent: most US and Canadian digital wealth managers charge at this level.

Stream 2 · Per-employee subscription

Pricing will be tiered, so that larger firms pay €7 per employee per month whereas smaller employers pay €3.50. The structure follows established HRIS subscription rates (Personio, BambooHR), and Kota uses the same per-employee structure on its pension brokerage platform.

Subscription serves as a predictable revenue line from day one, providing cash flow before AUM scales. In Year 2 it is a meaningful share of revenue, reducing to around 22% in Year 4 as AUM grows.

Stream 3 · Health and Life Insurance Commissions

Accruo resells group health and group life cover from established Irish carriers and earns a 6% commission on premiums, the standard rate across the Irish broker market. Average premiums are €1,500 per employee for health and €75 for life, yielding roughly €95 per employee in annual commission. Insurance contributes around 12% of total revenue at scale, and lets Accruo own the full employee-benefit relationship and develop an ecosystem that makes it less likely the customer will migrate.
