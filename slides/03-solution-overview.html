The Solution
2.1 · Solution overview

A benefits platform for HR teams. A pension app for the individual.

For HR teams, Accruo is a benefits provider. We offer a leading pension and partner with carriers for health and life cover, giving HR teams a unified benefits experience and their employees a single app to see it all. For individuals, Accruo is a pension provider that makes saving for your own retirement easy. Launching in Ireland first, we are designed to scale across Europe.

Why two customers

Building a product HR teams love means building a product their employees love. The employee experience is what makes the HR sale stick. Once that's built, targeting individuals directly makes sense for three reasons:

01
Their own market. It's a large market. It includes sole proprietors, company directors, and people who want to save for retirement outside their workplace plan. The numbers show these clients have larger portfolios.
02
Try-before-you-buy. HR directors can sign up for Accruo themselves and use the product before deciding for their company. This is made possible by our regulatory design, a differentiator from market incumbents.
03
Advocates. Individuals using Accruo will recommend it to colleagues and become ambassadors for the product inside their company.
Why tackle life and health cover too

HR admin is a major pain point, and we're setting out to solve it with a better pension. But pensions are just one component of a workplace benefits package, and the rest is low-hanging fruit. It would be remiss to fix one and leave the entire benefits headache untouched.

Our discussions with the COO of an Irish wealth management company
“There are 2,500 brokers in Ireland, and they're product-led, not benefit-value-led. We're behind the US on thinking in terms of a total compensation package.”

Ireland is behind other markets in integrating these three products. HR teams typically source pensions, health, and life cover from three different brokers, adding complexity at every step. We have an opportunity to be the all-in-one benefits provider, especially as the market shifts toward viewing benefits as part of a broader compensation package rather than three disconnected add-ons.
