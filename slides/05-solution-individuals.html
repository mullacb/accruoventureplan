Information, not advice

Factual questions get grounded answers. Advice-shaped questions get redirected.

01 · Onboard

Two minutes from invite to enrolled.

A WhatsApp or email invite, identity verified using payroll data already on file, a single fund matched to retirement date with the option to choose differently. Saving starts in minutes.

02 · Track

What saving is buying you.

The home screen shows what the employee has contributed, what tax they have saved, and what their employer has added, front and centre. The pension stops being a deduction; the employer's contribution is visibly attributed to the employer.

03 · Ask

Information surfaced, not advice given.

An in-app AI agent surfaces information grounded in the employee's own account and Irish regulation. The rail between information and advice is hard-coded, so when a question crosses it, the assistant says so.

Our discussion with an Irish charity HR lead
“Pensions aren't valued. Employees only really see them once a year, when the colourful benefits brochure is sent in the mail. The youngest employee in my office is trying to opt out, and I'm having to convince her against it.”

For the saver, this is the fix to the three failures from section 1: analogue admin becomes a two-minute signup, opacity becomes a home screen they actually understand, and poor value becomes the employer's contribution showing up as money they own.
