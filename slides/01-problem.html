1.1 · The Problem

A retirement crisis, and a competitiveness crisis. One root cause.

State pensions are under pressure. Ageing populations are straining pay-as-you-go systems, and governments are shifting the burden of retirement saving onto individuals. Yet only 20% of EU citizens participate in a workplace pension.1

Europe has a parallel challenge. The Draghi report on EU competitiveness identified an €800 billion annual investment gap Europe must close to fund its infrastructure and industrial renewal. The report argued this gap can only be closed by long-term retirement savings.2

Both problems point to the same fix. Europeans need retirement products that put their money to work, and today's don't deliver. Europeans save €1.4 trillion a year, but €10 trillion, around 70% of household savings, sits in low-yield deposits.3 The small share that does reach pensions is eroded by fees averaging 2% in Europe versus 0.26% for US 401(k)s,4 compounding into a 30% smaller retirement pot.5

The savings experience is why people aren't saving.

“When I joined a multinational in Ireland, nobody could explain my workplace pension. The broker took weeks to arrange a meeting. Fund options arrived on five separate sheets of paper with no fee comparison. Enrolment required scanning paper forms. And when my employer improved their contribution — effectively a pay rise — the change was buried in jargon nobody in my office understood. I thought, surely, this could be better.”

— Callum, Co-Founder of Accruo
