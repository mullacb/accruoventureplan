4.1 · Path to launch

The first year. Getting a licence.

This is the regulatory build year, focused on acquiring two licences in parallel: the MiFID Class 3 licence to build the pension, and the less burdensome Insurance Intermediary Authorisation to resell health and life insurance. For MiFID, the CBI targets a six-month decision from submission; the full path including pre-application engagement, documents, and review is around 327 days end-to-end.

What is required

The Central Bank of Ireland sets out a structured application process with clear deliverables at each step. Authorisation also requires the firm to staff a defined set of Pre-Approved Control Functions (PCFs). As is standard for smaller firms, we draw on our networks to fill these roles on a fractional basis, keeping costs lean while we build.

Two backstops if anything slips
Backstop 1 · Revenue path
Broker pensions on the IIA
The IIA we're acquiring for insurance also permits the resale of pensions from existing providers. If the MiFID timeline slips, we operate as a pension broker on the same licence until authorisation lands, so Year 1 still generates revenue.
Backstop 2 · Asset created
The licensed entity has shell value
A fully-authorised MiFID Class 3 firm in Ireland is itself a saleable asset. Year 1 spend creates both the build and a regulated entity that retains value if the product underperforms.
Engineering build-out

Engineering ramps in parallel with the regulatory build. Founders prototype the platform in early Y1, joined by a lead engineer in Q4 2026 who takes MVP development through to the July 2027 launch. The team scales to three engineers by Y2 and five by Y3, sized to ship the HR dashboard, employee app, and custody integrations Ireland needs, then to ready the platform for EuroPension in Europe.
