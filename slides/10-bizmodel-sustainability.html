3.2 · Sustainability

Built for where the market is going.

Three structural reasons the revenue base lasts. The broker fee model is on borrowed time. The customer travels with us through job changes. And direct distribution opens the larger end of the market that brokers cannot reach.

01 · Regulatory direction

The broker fee model is on borrowed time.

The UK has eliminated commissions on corporate pension schemes. Ireland trails the UK on pension reform by five to ten years and trends the same way. A model built on broker commissions is positioned for a fee structure that is being legislated away. Accruo is positioned for the post-broker market, not the current one.

02 · Customer continuity

The PRSA travels with the customer.

Workplace pensions are typically tied to the employer; when the employee leaves, a new provider inherits the assets. The PRSA is different: as a personal pension, it stays with the saver, and so does Accruo. The same structure opens cross-sell to the saver, which a broker cannot do because their relationship runs through HR, not the individual.

03 · Distribution reach

Direct distribution opens the larger market.

Schemes above the €100m threshold increasingly go direct in pursuit of lower fees, locking broker-dependent platforms out of the upper end of the market. Accruo's direct model and pricing work without a broker intermediary from day one. As Accruo gains marketplace credibility, larger schemes become reachable, multiplying the LTV per customer (see below).

A broker earns a margin the regulator can take away, on a customer who leaves when they change job, in a limited market segment. Accruo earns a margin that compounds for thirty years per saver, in the segments that are growing.
