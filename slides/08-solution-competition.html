08 · Competitive landscape Three competitor types, three constraints
2.6 · Competitive landscape

Three competitor types. Each blocked by a different structural constraint.

The Irish workplace pension market today has digital brokers, legacy manufacturer-providers, and a small set of European digital pension players. Accruo competes against all three. Each is held back by a constraint they cannot solve without rebuilding the business they have.

01 · Digital brokers

Insurance brokers in digital wrappers.

Kota (Series A 2025) and Zen Pensions (launched Jan 2026) resell PRSAs from manufacturers like Acorn or Aviva. They take a broker commission plus an AUM charge. They do not manufacture the product, so they cannot price below their providers. The fee structure is capped by the model.

02 · Legacy providers

Manufacturers caught between technology and channel.

Irish Life, Zurich, and New Ireland manufacture Irish pensions but distribute through brokers that account for roughly 80% of their revenue. A direct digital channel disintermediates their own distributors. The technology gap is solvable; the channel conflict is not.

03 · European digital

Sub-scale, domestic, or not EuroPension.

Finax (Slovakia) is the only EuroPension provider with material AUM, and is B2C-only. LifeGoals (Cyprus) targets cross-border expats. Epsor (France) and Xempus (Germany) are domestic B2B platforms that do not offer EuroPension. None compete in the Irish workplace segment.

Acquisition signal · the UK precedent
NatWest acquired Cushon
£144M · 2023
UK workplace savings and pensions fintech, acquired by the banking group to access modern pension UX, a pre-built licence, and a younger customer base.
JPMorgan acquired WealthOS
undisclosed · 2026
UK pensions administration and wealth-tech platform, acquired to gain pre-built infrastructure rather than build it from scratch internally.
Incumbents are turning to acquisitions to close the technology gap they cannot close organically. The pattern began in the UK and is expected to extend to Ireland and continental Europe as digital-first pension entrants reach scale.

The conclusion: Accruo competes against three categories of player, none of whom is positioned to match the structural choices in slide 2.5 without breaking their existing model. The competitive response so far has been acquisition, not imitation.
