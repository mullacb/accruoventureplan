02 · Why Now & Market The window, and the prize
1.2 · Why now

The window just opened. Two policy moments, six months apart.

1 January 2026

Auto-enrolment launched in Ireland.

Ireland's new auto-enrolment scheme brings 800,000 workers into a pension for the first time.6 Employers now contribute by default, and a state-led awareness campaign is bringing pensions into every Irish workplace conversation. The question is no longer whether to offer a pension, but which platform to offer.

20 November 2025

The EU reformed its EuroPension product.

EuroPension accounts (the Pan-European Personal Pension, or PEPP) are a standardised individual pension, portable across the EU under a single regulatory licence. The Commission's reforms scrap mandatory advice, require tax parity with domestic products, and open EuroPension for use in workplace auto-enrolment.7 A firm licensed in Ireland today can passport into every EU market. And even if the reforms stall, Ireland, France, and Italy already permit employers to contribute directly into an employee's individual pension account, making Accruo's workplace model viable today.

The market compounds. Irish pension AUM grows roughly 10% a year from contributions and returns. The individual PRSA segment alone more than doubled between 2019 and 2024, from €7.3bn to €18.3bn.9 Auto-enrolment adds 800,000 contributors on top.

Then the licence travels. EuroPension accounts make the same product portable across the EU, and no provider has yet brought them to France or Italy. France's PER market grew 19% in the year to Q3 2025, reaching €141bn across 12.7 million holders.10 Italy, which already runs auto-enrolment, holds €243bn across 10 million workers.11 The platform needs only a consistent slice in each country.
