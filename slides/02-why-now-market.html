02 · Why Now & Market The window, and the prize
1.2 · Why now

Two reforms, six months apart. Demand in Ireland, a passport across Europe.

20 November 2025

EU announces reforms to the EuroPension.

EuroPension is a standardised retirement account. Any EU citizen can open one, and any firm licensed in one member state can sell it to customers in any other. One product, one licence, 27 markets, 450 million savers. Launched in 2019, it's being reformed to make it an even more competitive product.6 Even unreformed, the account is usable today. Ireland, France, Italy and others already permit direct employer contributions into the accounts, making it viable across three large workplace savings markets.

1 January 2026

Auto-enrolment launched in Ireland.

Ireland's new auto-enrolment scheme brings 800,000 workers into a pension for the first time.7 Employers now contribute by default, and a state-led awareness campaign is bringing pensions into every Irish workplace conversation. The question is no longer whether to offer a pension, but which platform to offer.

The market compounds. Irish pension assets under management (AUM) grow over 10% a year from contributions and investment returns. The individual PRSA segment alone more than doubled between 2019 and 2024, from €7.3bn to €18.3bn.9 Auto-enrolment adds 800,000 contributors on top.

Outside Ireland the market is less developed, but it makes up for it with size. In France, the market grew 19% YoY to Q3 2025, reaching €141bn across 12.7 million holders, and these accounts aren't even mandatory.10 It's similar in Italy, which has partial auto-enrolment, where €243bn is held across 10 million workers.11 The market is massive, and will grow massively as more governments move to auto-enrolment.
