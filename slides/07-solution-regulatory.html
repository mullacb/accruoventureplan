2.5 · Regulatory architecture

Three regulatory choices that shape the product. MiFID over insurance. Individual over collective. EU over domestic.

Most of what slides 2.1 through 2.4 describe, the unified platform, the low fees, the cross-border account, the path to self-direction, follows from three regulatory choices made early. Each is a fork from how Irish pension incumbents are structured. Together they determine what the product can do later.

01 · MiFID, not insurance

An investment account, not an insurance contract.

Accruo's pension is a MiFID custody account holding funds on behalf of the saver. Incumbents structure pensions as unit-linked life-insurance products, a relic from when pensions really were old-age insurance. The MiFID wrapper unlocks broad fund choice, transparent fees, and a path to self-direction that insurance-based products cannot offer without rebuilding from the ground up.

02 · Individual, not collective

The saver owns the account. The employer contributes.

Each employee holds their own pension account; the employer's contributions flow into it. Incumbents run collective company schemes where employees own a share of a pooled pot managed by the employer. Accruo's account moves with the employee across jobs, providers, and borders. Almost no one is leveraging this structure today.

03 · EuroPension, not domestic

A licence that travels across the EU.

Authorised under the EU's EuroPension (PEPP) regulation, the Irish licence operates in every member state without re-licensing; only the local tax wrapper changes. Domestic-licensed incumbents need separate authorisation in every market they enter, which is why so few of them ever do.

2.5 continued · What this means competitively

An incumbent can copy any one of these. Doing all three rebuilds their entire business.

Switching the wrapper from insurance to MiFID requires a new product, a new licence, and a new operations stack. Switching from collective to individual accounts breaks the model their broker distribution is built around. Switching from domestic to EuroPension requires authorisation in every market. Each is a multi-year programme. Doing all three at once requires building from a green field.

Slide 2.6 shows how this plays out across the three competitor types in the market today, and why none of them have moved.
