2.5 · Regulatory design

Regulatory design at the core.

Regulatory design is at the core of our product offering: from leveraging personal pension products (PRSA and EuroPension) in the workplace context, instead of the traditional group plans, to the choice between investment-account versus insurance wrapper, to the location we launch from.

01 · MiFID, not insurance

An investment account, not an insurance contract.

Accruo's pension is a MiFID custody account holding funds on behalf of the saver. Incumbents structure pensions as unit-linked life-insurance products. The MiFID wrapper unlocks broad fund choice, transparent fees, and a path to self-direction that insurance-based products cannot offer without rebuilding from the ground up.

02 · Individual, not collective

The saver owns the account. The employer contributes.

Each employee holds their own pension account; the employer's contributions flow into it. Incumbents run collective company schemes where employees own a share of a pooled pot managed by the employer. Accruo's account moves with the employee across jobs, providers, and borders.

03 · Personal pension as workplace product

The PRSA, used in the workplace context.

The PRSA is structurally a personal pension. We use it as the workplace product instead of the traditional group plan. The same structure, applied across the EU, is the EuroPension: the choice that works in Ireland is the choice that also passports into every member state, with only the local tax wrapper changing.

Why Ireland over Lithuania

We evaluated Ireland against startup-friendly jurisdictions like Lithuania. We chose Ireland for three reasons: an improving regulatory environment, a competitive corporate tax structure, and a promising initial market with the auto-enrolment tailwind launching this year.
