4.3 · The expansion playbook

Pension is the wedge. The platform is the destination.

After the Irish launch, expansion runs in three sequential phases. Each phase reuses the infrastructure of the previous one: the next country reuses the licence stack, the next product reuses the custody integration, the next adjacency reuses the customer relationship. Capital efficiency of expansion is the moat against well-funded entrants.

Three phases · what comes after July 2027
Phase 1 · Y2 to Y3

Passport across the EU.

France first. Three reasons in this order: fastest-growing EU pension market by net flows; native French capacity on the founding team; no Kota equivalent in market. PEPP's design means the entry bar is tax-status approval, not a fresh licence — meaningfully lower than starting over. Sequence: tax filing Q3 2027, distribution partnerships Q4 2027, French launch H1 2028. Italy and Germany follow.

Phase 2 · Y3 to Y4

Adjacent products on the same licence.

Ireland's incoming Savings & Investment Account (the EU's ISA-equivalent under SIU discussion) drops onto the same custody stack with limited new infrastructure. The customisation upgrade moves the non-standard PRSA from fixed fund menus to client-pick. A digital advice layer follows once authorisation and product data both support it.

Phase 3 · Y4+

Wealth-creation ecosystem.

Banking and lending in-app, served to a customer cohort already paying for pension. Internalised insurance built off the claims data observed on the platform, replacing the carriers Accruo currently resells. Expanded HR-side benefits: spending cards, gym memberships, travel partnerships, contribution-linked employee rewards. Each adjacency is an additional revenue line on a base that already exists.

Why this sequence. France reuses the Ireland licence stack. SIA reuses the custody integration. Banking reuses the customer relationship. Each phase is a deployment of existing infrastructure, not a rebuild -- the same compounding logic Section 2 applied to product applies again to expansion.

Every quarter the platform exists, the cost of leaving for a competitor goes up by another product.

The pension is the entry point, not the destination. Once a saver consolidates their old pots, sets their contribution rate, adds health and life cover, and (eventually) takes a loan or holds savings against their balance, the relationship is not a pension provider relationship. It is the relationship every neobank wishes it had: long-dated, salary-linked, and tax-advantaged. That is the ecosystem the platform is being built towards.
